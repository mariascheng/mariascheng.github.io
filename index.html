Student, organizer, academic.

My name is Maria Cheng, and I am a rising junior at St. John’s School in Houston, Texas. I attend the Oregon State University as a non-degree seeking student in the College of Liberal Arts for the 2020 Summer term. I’m particularly interested in investigating political methodology, statistics in public policy, and environmental justice in the United States.

Justice advocate, day and night.

I have an extensive background working in social and political justice advocacy; I currently serve as a National Educational Facilitator and Texas State Lead with the US Youth Climate Strike. In my experience, I have worked with various grassroots organizations, including the Sunrise Movement, Sierra Club, March for Our Lives, and the ACLU. I am a research assistant for the Arizona State University’s School of Policy and Global Studies in the summer of 2020, investigating lobbying, U.S. state politics, and federal legislation.

Proud Texan & global citizen.

At home, I work as a summer volunteer at the Houston Museum of Natural Science and am an active part in my community. I am a two-time Presidential Volunteer Service Gold Award recipient, one-time American Invitational Mathematics Examination qualifier, and two-time Texas Forensics Association state tournament qualifier. I am an alumnus of the 2020 RunningStart High School Program and 2020 ACLU National Advocacy Institute.
